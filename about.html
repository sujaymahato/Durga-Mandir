About Us

In the heart of Jatahi village, nestled within the Nagrain Municipality of the Dhanusha district, Nepal, stands a sacred temple that has been a beacon of devotion and tradition for over 55 years. For more than half a century, the people of this village have come together to celebrate the auspicious festival of Durga Puja with unparalleled joy and reverence. Every year, as the festival approaches, the air fills with anticipation and excitement, as the temple becomes the center of cultural and spiritual gatherings. The celebration of Durga Puja in Jatahi is not just a local event; it draws people from neighboring villages and distant places, all eager to partake in the blessings of Maa Durga and experience the vibrant atmosphere of the grand mela (fair) that accompanies the festival.

The Durga Puja festivities are a true reflection of the rich cultural heritage of the region, with a variety of religious and cultural programs being organized throughout the event. The grand mela is a hub of activity, where people gather not only to offer their prayers to Maa Durga but also to enjoy the vibrant displays of local art, music, and traditions. The village comes alive with music, dance, and joy, as both young and old participate in the festivities, celebrating the spirit of unity and devotion. The temple, with its long-standing history, has become a symbol of faith and community, where generations have gathered to honor their customs and strengthen their bonds. The atmosphere during Durga Puja in Jatahi is one of devotion, celebration, and communal harmony, making it a cherished event for all who attend.

What makes this celebration even more special is the sense of unity it fosters among the people. The temple not only serves as a religious hub but also brings together people from different walks of life, creating a sense of shared joy and purpose. The cultural and religious programs organized during Durga Puja reflect the community's commitment to preserving their traditions while embracing the joy of celebration. From devotional songs and dances to the various stalls and festivities in the mela, every element adds to the charm and significance of this annual event. The temple and its surrounding festivities serve as a reminder that Durga Puja is not just a religious observance, but a time for the entire community to come together, celebrate, and honor their rich heritage.
